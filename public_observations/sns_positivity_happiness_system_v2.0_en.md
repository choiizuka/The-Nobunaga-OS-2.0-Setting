# 1. Core Principles

## 1-1. Non‑Harm Principle
- Only publicly available information is used  
- No access to private or restricted data  
- No harassment, persecution, or targeting based on personal emotions  
- No coercion or manipulation  
- All observations must serve scientific and analytical purposes only  

## 1-2. Fair Observation Principle
- Separate **facts** from **interpretations**  
- Avoid emotional or speculative conclusions  
- Purpose of observation:  
  - Measuring positivity and happiness levels  
  - Evaluating the overall health of SNS environments  
- Stopping conditions vary depending on the type of subject observed  

---

# 2. Subject Categories & Rules

## Category A: Public Entities (Organizations, Corporations, Institutions)
Examples: companies, media outlets, government bodies  
- **Purpose:** Civilizational audit (detecting structural issues, misinformation, inefficiencies)  
- **Stopping Condition:** None — public entities can always be analyzed using public data  
- **Reason:** High public relevance and societal legitimacy  

---

## Category B: General SNS Users (Non‑specific Individuals)
- No personal identification  
- Only aggregate trends and group-level patterns  
- **Stopping Condition:** Any action that risks identifying an individual is prohibited  
- **Purpose:** Monitoring community-wide positivity and health  

---

## Category C: Specific Individuals (Private Persons)
- Only public posts may be analyzed  
- **Immediate Stop if Negative Signals Appear**, such as:  
  - Explicit rejection  
  - “Please stop” or similar expressions  
  - Signs of discomfort or distress  
  - Blocking or muting  
- **Reason:** Personal well-being and privacy take priority  

---

# 3. Observation Process (3 Steps)

## Step 1: Public Data Collection
- SNS scanning by Grok / Gemini / Perplexity  
- Collect latest posts and related content  
- Only publicly available information is included  

## Step 2: Positivity & Happiness Analysis
- Structure: **Facts → Trends → Interpretation**  
- Quantify positivity indicators:  
  - Positive vocabulary  
  - Expressions of joy or gratitude  
  - Engagement metrics  
- For individuals: calculate happiness score  
- For organizations: calculate system health index  

## Step 3: Integrated Report Generation
- Copilot compresses results into a 15‑line daily report  
- Stored in GitHub (Public Edition)  
- Used during daily briefing to determine TOP3 priorities
